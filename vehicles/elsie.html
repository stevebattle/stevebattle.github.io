Interactive ELSIE

ELSIE: Electro-mechanical robot, Light Sensitive with Internal (battery) and External (scanner and shell) stability. Her behaviours are:

  • E: Exploration
  • P: Positive phototropism
  • N: Negative phototropism with >50% battery
  • O: Obstacle avoidance
  • R: Recharging at the light source

Click on the window to add and remove obstacles.

In 'Principles of Robot Autonomy', I argue that ELSIEs autonomy is constituted as an organisationally closed graph; a model of her underlying state-machine. This is a practical application of the work of Francisco Varela's, 'Principles of Biological Autonomy.'

"Not in looks, but in action, the model must resemble an animal. Therefore it must have these or some measure of these attributes: exploration, curiosity, free-will in the sense of unpredictability, goalseeking, self-regulation, avoidance of dilemmas, foresight, memory, learning, forgetting, association of ideas, form recognition, and the elements of social accommodation. Such is life."

W. Grey Walter, The Living Brain, 1953.

Braitenberg Vehicle #6: Selection, the Impersonal Engineer

We apply Darwinian selection to vehicles. New vehicles are created by random mutation, and only those that remain in the window are fit to survive.

The model used here is based on W. Ross Ashby's Homeostat described in 'Design for a Brain'. In chapter 9/16 Ashby describes a mobile Homeostat, U, "suppose U is mobile and is ultrastable, with its critical states set so that it seeks situations of high illumination." The illustration on the vehicle is a representation of the Homeostat, R, engaged with its environment, maintaining an essential variable, S, it's proximity to the light.

"Where there has been no conscious engineering at all, as in the case of our type 6 vehicles, analysis will necessarily produce the feeling of a mysterious supernatural hand guiding the creation."

Valentino Braitenberg

See Braitenberg, V. (1984). Vehicles: Experiments in synthetic psychology. Cambridge, MA: MIT Press

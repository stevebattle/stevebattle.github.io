Braitenberg Vehicle #5: Logic

Vehicle 4 introduces new internal nodes that represent artificial neurons, analogous to our own brain cells. These have a non-linear activation function that controls how much the output is activated by its inputs.

Vehicle 5a has a feed-forward neural network, where the output of one neuron can be the input to another neuron. The main restriction is that these connections must not form a cycle, so it can only react to it's input.

Vehicle 5b introduces recurrent excitatory connections in a Continuous Time Recurrent Neural Network. A pair of mutually inhibitory neurons oscillate to form a Matsuoka oscillator, which is used here to control the scanning of the proximity sensor.

Vehicle 5c looks at recurrent excitatory connections. An excitatory autapse appears where a neuron loops back on itself. This creates a positive feedback loop which acts as a neural monostable, which is used here to prolong the momentary stimulus of contact with the vehicle shell long enough for it to reverse clear of the obstacle.

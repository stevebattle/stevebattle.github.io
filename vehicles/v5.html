Braitenberg Vehicle #5: Logic

To make a brain out of artificial neurons we can connect them together whichever way we like.

Vehicle 5a has a feed-forward neural network, where the output of one neuron can be the input to another neuron. The only restriction is that these connections must not form a cycle, so it can only react to input.

Vehicle 5b introduces recurrent inhibitory connections in a Continuous Time Recurrent Neural Network. A pair of mutually inhibitory neurons work together as a Matsuoka oscillator, used here to control the scanning sensor.

Vehicle 5c looks at recurrent excitatory connections. An excitatory autapse is where a neuron loops back on itself, creating a positive feedback loop that acts as a neural monostable. This sustains the momentary stimulus of contact long enough for the vehicle to clear the obstacle.

"This is an elementary sort of MEMORY. It is not difficult to understand how out of such elementary memory stores (consisting of such reciprocally connected threshold devices) complex memories can be synthesised, with the possibility of storing extremely complex events."

Valentino Braitenberg
